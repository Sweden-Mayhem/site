# You can die now, and come back

This was a big stretch of work, so this is a big update. The short version: dying no longer ruins your character, the opening tutorial now has its full cast of teachers and its first quest, Mileth has been checked door by door, and we went through the plumbing under the hood so groups, mail, and the interface hold up. The longer version follows.

## Dying no longer breaks you

This is the big one. Until now, the first time anything killed you, your character was effectively bricked. The moment your health hit zero you became a ghost that could not move, could not act, and had no way back to the living. People hit this and simply could not play. That is gone.

Death works now. When you fall, the cold chill of Sgrios takes you, the god of death sets his scar on your legend, and you lose a small slice of experience (five percent, and none at all while you are still a newbie under level five). Then you are carried back to the living at the Mileth temple, restored to full health, with a holy chime as you wake:

> You awaken in the temple at Mileth, bearing the scar of Sgrios.

The scar is written into your legend, the same place your birth and your deeds are recorded:

> Scarred by Sgrios, the God of Destruction

A couple of caveats, since we would rather say so. This is the core of the system, not all of it. A friend reviving you on the spot with a Red Potion, the visible ghost and skull, the walk through Sgrios's realm, and a death pile of dropped items are all still to come. But the part that matters most, that death no longer takes your character out of your hands, is done.

## The tutorial has its real cast now

The opening area, the Path of Beginnings, has been rebuilt from a rough stand-in into the proper tutorial. The biggest change is the cast. The single invented guide we had before is retired, and in his place stand five teachers, each one teaching a single thing:

| Teacher | What they teach |
|---|---|
| Raghnall | Combat, and how to swing at something |
| Bartley | Reading your status and the world around you |
| Myles | A wizard, who grants the practice spell Srad Tut |
| Eadaoin | Talking to other Aislings and forming groups (and she runs the apple shop) |
| Keelin | Reading signs and message boards, beside her grey slate |

Myles does more than talk. He grants Srad Tut, a weak fire spell that only ever exists inside the tutorial, so you have something to practice casting before you leave. It is deliberately feeble, one small fire hit for a sliver of mana.

Eadaoin runs the apple shop: buy an apple for five gold, sell one back for two. Keelin stands beside her grey slate, the board that anyone can read but only Demagogues may post to, and we seeded it with a few words of counsel.

### Your first quest: Gerard's floppies

We also built the tutorial's first quest, which meant building the machinery for quests that count what you kill. Gerard the gardener asks you to clear his garden to the north of one male floppy (white) and one female floppy (brown). A new path leads up into that garden, now full of hopping floppies. Bring down one of each and return to him for 600 gold and a mark on your legend. Strike the right floppies and you watch your progress tick up; the practice floppies back on the main field do not count, so there is no shortcutting it.

### A calmer, tidier field

Two more fixes round out the area. The mantis that used to wander the tutorial did not belong (it came from a different game's data), so it is gone. And the practice creatures are now where you can find them: the training dummies stand right next to Raghnall, the combat teacher, and the floppies gather in a small grove just north of the teacher row instead of scattered thin across the whole map.

Finally, the gate out works again. Grendwald's "step through the gate" choice had quietly become a dead button that did nothing. Now it does what it says and sends you on to the Mileth Inn.

Still to come here: Flynn the basement combat trainer, the proper multi-room building, and the Tutorial Book reader. But all five teachers, the spell, the first quest, and a safe practice field are in.

## Mileth, measured to the inch

Mileth is the first town, so we checked every connection door by door. All 38 of its building doors, shop entrances, both inn doors, and the stairs down into the crypt land exactly where they should. We traced the whole crypt staircase, from the town square down through the vestibule into Mileth Crypt 1, and it lines up end to end.

We also put the Mileth Village message board back at its corner, so you can read and leave postings in town.

A few things are deliberately still on the list, and we would rather name them. A handful of warps point to maps we cannot build yet (the Temple of Choosing and the Training Room) because they need art we do not have. And the Mileth Altar is real, but it is endgame, so it waits its turn.

## Under the hood: groups, mail, and the interface

Not all of this was visible. We went through the core social systems to confirm they hold up. The full interface (every button and action the client can send), grouping (invites, the recruiting box, kick and promote, and shared experience and loot inside a party), and mail (reading and sending, with your mailbox carried right in the boards list) are all handled and working on the server. What remains for these is a live two-person test in the game, which we will run.

One real bug fell out of that check: a leftover developer test board was quietly showing up in everyone's board and mail list. It is removed, so your list is clean again.

## What's next

We keep walking the list in order: for the tutorial, Flynn and the proper building; for Mileth, the townsfolk with real things to say and the first town quests; and a live two-player test of the social systems.
