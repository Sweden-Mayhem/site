# The tutorial stops fighting back

If you made a brand new character recently and got mobbed by your own practice targets, sorry about that, and it is fixed. The training dummies in the opening area used to wander off and swing at you, and the little floppies would chase a level one peasant around the field. The start of the game is meant to be a calm place to find your feet, so now it is one.

Here is what happens now. The training dummies stand still, the way a dummy should, and wait for you to practice your swing. The floppies hop gently around the field, and you can hunt them for a little experience. None of them, dummy or floppy, will turn on you, chase you, or hit back when you strike first.

The cause was small but stubborn. Telling a creature to ignore you on sight is not the same as telling it to never fight back, and our setup only did the first half, so the moment a new player took a practice swing, the creature decided it had a quarrel after all. We wrote two proper behaviours instead, one for inert training dummies and one for docile creatures that wander but never raise a hand. Both are reusable, so any peaceful critter in the world can use them later.

While we were in here we mapped out the rest of this area, and there is a good stretch of road ahead for it: the tutorial teachers, a gardener named Gerard with a quest to thin out his garden, the proper building, and the Tutorial Book. Those are coming.

For now, the very first thing you touch in the game is a little gentler. Make a character and go knock a floppy or two around. Nothing will knock back.
