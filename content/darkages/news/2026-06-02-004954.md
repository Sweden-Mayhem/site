# The tutorial stops fighting back

If you made a brand new character lately and got mobbed by your own practice targets, we owe you an apology and a fix. The training dummies in the opening area used to wander off and swing at you, and the little floppies would chase a level one peasant around the field. That is not how anyone remembers learning to play, so it is gone.

Here is what happens now. The training dummies stand still, the way a dummy should, and wait for you to practice your swing. The floppies hop gently around the field like the bunny shaped creatures they have always been, and you can hunt them for a little experience. None of them, dummy or floppy, will turn and attack you, chase you, or hit back when you strike first. The opening of the game is a calm place to find your footing again.

The cause was small but stubborn. Telling a creature to ignore you on sight is not the same as telling it to never fight back, and the old setup only did the first half. So the moment a new player took a practice swing, the creature decided it had a quarrel after all. We built two proper behaviors instead, one for inert training dummies and one for docile creatures that wander but never raise a hand. Both are reusable, so any peaceful critter in the world can borrow them later.

Digging into this also let us check the old records, and we are happy to report the floppies are exactly right. The real tutorial did hand you off to a small forest of floppies to learn combat, with a gardener named Gerard who asked you to clear a male and a female from his garden. That quest, the tutorial teachers, the proper building, and the Tutorial Book are the next stretch of road for this area.

For now, the very first thing a returning player touches feels a little more like home. Make a character and go knock a floppy or two around. Nothing will knock back.
