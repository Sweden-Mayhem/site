# Down past the first crypt floor, and a kit for every path

The last update rebuilt the first floor of the Mileth Crypt and gave each path the start of something to learn. This time we kept walking down the stairs, finished the wizard's elements top to bottom, handed the Rogue and the Monk their own opening moves, and spent a good while making the tutorial and Mileth read true. Here is the run-down.

## The crypt now runs eight floors deep

Until now only the first floor of the Mileth Crypt had been built out. Floors two through eight were still a rough import: numbered creatures like "Spider5", stats wildly out of place for the depth, and nothing that fought with more than its teeth. That is sorted. Descend and the company changes the way it should, getting nastier the lower you go:

| Floor | Creatures |
|---|---|
| 2 | Rats, Spiders, Centipedes |
| 3 | Spiders, Centipedes, Bats |
| 4 | Spiders, Centipedes, Bats |
| 5 | Centipedes, Bats, Scorpions |
| 6 | Centipedes, Bats, Scorpions |
| 7 | Bats, Scorpions, Great Bats |
| 8 | Bats, Scorpions, Great Bats |

The timid rats and spiders of the upper floors give way to centipedes that hunt on sight, then bats, then stinging scorpions, then great bats.

And for the first time, the crypt answers with more than teeth.

![A crypt bat](/darkages/images/crypt-bat.png)

A Bat screeches a bolt of darkness across the room, and a Great Bat a worse one, and that darkness bites hard against an unguarded Aisling. Casting turned out to be something the engine could already do once a creature was simply handed a spell to know, so expect more spellcasters as we go down. If a bat ever fails to get its screech off it falls to biting instead, so it is never harmless.

Their drops land where they belong now, too. Bats give up a Bat's Wing, scorpions a Scorpion's Sting, great bats a Great Bat Wing, all of them reagents Dar pays for in town. You can finally find them on the floors they are meant to be found on.

## A starter kit for all five paths

The Warrior, Wizard, and Priest each got the start of a kit last time. Now the other two paths catch up, so every class has something of its own to learn around level five.

Rogues get **Stab** and, a little later, **Stab Twice**, the close-in dagger work the path is built around. Two of the Rogue's signature tools, **Hide** and **Needle Trap**, were also quietly broken: both had shipped with no target to act on, so Hide hid nobody and Needle Trap laid nothing. Both work now. Hide drops you out of sight for about two minutes, until you strike or fiddle with something, and Needle Trap leaves a nasty surprise on the ground for the first foe to step on it.

Monks were the last path with nothing class-defining to train beyond the universal Assail. They now open with **Kick**, then **High Kick** once they have a little Kick under their belt, then **Double Punch**, a strike with a chance to land twice. With that, all five paths (Warrior, Rogue, Wizard, Priest, Monk) have a real opening kit.

All of these are learnable from Kousai, the Mileth trainer, and each shows up only for the class it belongs to.

## The wizard's elements, top to bottom

A Wizard's four elements now run the full ladder. The lesser and base forms went in last time; this update adds the greater (**Mor**) and astounding (**Ard**) tiers for all four of Srad, Sal, Athar, and Creag. That is sixteen spells, and a wizard now has the whole climb ahead of them.

Each spell's damage scales with the caster's Intellect. The figures below are the base damage and the multiplier on Intellect:

| Tier | Level | Mana | Base | x Intellect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beag | 2 | 6 | 10 | 2 |
| base | 8 | 15 | 20 | 3 |
| Mor | 41 | 40 | 40 | 5 |
| Ard | 75 | 100 | 80 | 8 |

Each tier asks for more of its element's reagent than the last and wants the form below it learned first, so it is a real climb. The damage and mana numbers are our own balance for now and may shift once they get played.

## More prayers for priests

The Priest's healing and curse work both grew. On the mending side, **Mor Ioc** and **Ard Ioc** close far deeper wounds than the early prayers, for when a small heal no longer keeps pace. On the other side, a fuller curse line: **Cradh** and **Mor Cradh** strip more and more armour off a foe so they take heavier hits, each paired with a cleansing prayer, **Ao Cradh** and **Ao Mor Cradh**, that lifts the curse off an ally. That brings the Priest to thirteen prayers, the core of the healer and curse-breaker the path is meant to be.

One prayer we did not fake: Dion, the shield meant to make you all but untouchable for a moment, needs something our engine cannot do cleanly yet, so it is set aside rather than shipped as a feeble imitation.

## The tutorial, taking shape

The opening is filling in. **Flynn**, a combat trainer, now stands among the other teachers and gives a brand new character their first real fight and their first coins: harmless mice wander a practice patch, drop **Small Mold** when slain, and Flynn buys each clump for five gold. He rounds out the full set of tutorial teachers.

Those teachers used to stand dumped in a straight line across an empty floor. They are now spread across the ground where each one belongs: the greeters near where you appear, the combat trainers beside the practice pens they watch, the priestess at the message board she teaches, the gardener up by the path to his garden, each turned to face the thing it tends.

The garden got a cleanup of its own. Gerard's floppy hunt asks you to tell a male floppy from a female one, so the garden now holds exactly those two, white and brown, both simply named Floppy, with the odd extra colours that used to wander there cleared out. The floppy population no longer creeps upward the longer the server runs, either.

## Mileth, made true to itself

Walk into Mileth out of the inn and the square is the right square now. The dozen invented townsfolk with their made-up surnames and the two guards who never belonged there are gone, and in their place stand the two figures who actually live on that map: a **Beggar** by the east edge and **Locky** near the middle.

The inn you wake in got the same treatment. **Riona** the innkeeper now stands behind her counter where she should, the rat underfoot carries its proper name, and a drunk called Artur who had been planted there by a bad import has been shown the door.

Mileth also gained its **White Magic Master, Devlin**, who had been stranded off in an empty, unreachable room and so was never met at all. She stands in the square now and offers the quest **On Honey**: bring her raw wax or honey from the woodland bees for 10,000 experience, repeatable every twelve hours. One catch we will name plainly. The bees live out in the Eastern Woods, and that stretch of countryside does not yet connect to Mileth by road, so the quest is written and ready but not yet walkable end to end. Linking the fields between towns is its own job further down the list.

## Where this sits

Most of the above was built and checked on the server. It builds clean, the data loads, every dialog leads somewhere real, and the crypt's casting chain traces all the way through. The next job is to walk each of these through in the live game, double-clicking each NPC, casting each spell, killing each bat, and we will flag anything that misbehaves.

A few deeper things are still openly unfinished:

- A weapon's damage and accuracy still do not feed into combat, so for now your strength drives your hits. We have worked out how this ought to behave and put the last piece, a balance call, in front of the team to decide.
- Attacks never miss yet. There is no dodge or saving throw in the math.
- Gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things they have no business wearing.
- The crypt below floor eight is still the rough import, not yet brought up to the same standard as the floors above it.

All of it is on the list.
