# Now you can look around, and tell the time

This update is a string of smaller touches rather than one headline. Every Aisling now starts with two handy little abilities, the Mileth Inn became the first town room we can call finished, strong drink and spoiled food finally bite when you swallow them, the church gained its keeper, and we went over the first crypt floors again to get the details right. Here is the run-down.

## Two tricks every Aisling now carries

New characters now wake with two small abilities already in hand, no teacher needed.

![The Look skill](/darkages/images/look-skill.png) ![The Nis spell](/darkages/images/nis-spell.png)

**Look** inspects whatever sits in the tile directly in front of you. Face a patch of ground and it names the place you are standing. Face another Aisling and it reads off the weapon and armor they are wearing. Face an item dropped on the floor and it tells you what it is.

**Nis** is a tiny spell that tells you the hour and the date in the old Temuairan reckoning. Cast it and a line appears along the top of your screen:

> In Temuair it is the third sun of the sixth moon; the hour is 09:14 (morning).

Neither one changes a fight, but both are the sort of thing your hands reach for without thinking once you have had them a while, so every character carries them from the first step.

## The Mileth Inn is finished

The room you wake into after the opening, the Mileth Inn, is the first town map we are willing to call done. The innkeeper, Riona, now does everything she ought to.

She greets you, and a short exchange the first time you speak earns you 500 experience. She will note the day of your birth into your legend if you ask. She sells the Tutorial Book over the counter and buys it back if you are done with it, and that book is no longer a dead object in your pack: open it and it reads as a short beginner's guide across a few pages. She can also teach a Peasant to swim, though we will say plainly that there is no water event in the world to swim through yet, so for the moment it is a skill waiting for its purpose.

![Riona, the Mileth innkeeper](/darkages/images/riona.png)

One more small thing about that first room: the rat that shares it with you was still squaring up for a fight. It is a harmless creature now, the way the very first room should be, so a brand new Aisling has nothing to worry about while they find their feet.

## Mind what you drink

Last time, food and the healing draughts started doing their job, and we noted that the spoiled things were still inert. The other half of that is in now. Strong drink and rotten food bite when you swallow them.

![A bottle of Brandy](/darkages/images/brandy.png)

A swig of brandy, rum, or wine drains your health, and so does eating something gone off. None of it can kill you outright, though. The drain floors at a single point of health, so the worst a bad bottle does is leave you reeling.

| What you swallow | Health lost |
|---|---|
| Wine | 10 |
| Moldy Bread Slice | 10 |
| Rotten Tomato | 10 |
| Brandy | 20 |
| Rotten Vegetable | 20 |
| Rum | 40 |

While we were on the shelves, a few goods the Mileth vendors should have stocked are now in place. Brody the tailor sells a plain Shirt for 100 coins, Oona the baker a fresh Vegetable for 5 that mends a little health, Aingeal pours the brandy above for 200, and Dar carries a blank Parchment Scroll at 500. Cian the alchemist also takes two more of the oddments you drag up from the crypt, a moldy slice of bread and a rotten tomato, so there is a little more coin in clearing the place out.

![A plain Shirt](/darkages/images/shirt.png)

## A keeper for the church

The Mileth Church had nobody tending it. Now **Naomhan**, the chapel assistant, stands at the altar at the front of the nave. Speak to him and he will tell you about the Altar of Mileth, where an Aisling may lay an offering before the Gods in hopes of a blessing and where many a couple has been wed, and about the church itself. The offering and the weddings are lore he speaks of for now, not things you can do yet, but the altar has its herald. A stand-in figure the import had left loitering in the pews is gone.

We also cleared a stray produce stall out of the town square, since apples and food are Oona's trade over in the restaurant and there was no reason for a second seller standing in the open.

## Back through the crypt

We walked the first dungeon again and tidied up the details. The keeper standing at the mouth of the crypt is **Skan**, who tells you what waits below and who buys what you carry up. We had the wrong name on him before.

![A crypt Mouse](/darkages/images/mouse.png) ![A crypt Spider](/darkages/images/spider.png)

The little vermin on the upper floors are Mice, and their pickings now differ floor by floor the way they should. The mice on the first floor turn up mold, a rotten vegetable, or a moldy slice of bread. Go one floor down and the mice carry rotten apples, grapes, tomatoes, and cherries instead.

| Creature | What it drops |
|---|---|
| Mouse, floor one | Mold, Rotten Vegetable, Moldy Bread Slice |
| Mouse, floor two | Rotten Apple, Rotten Grapes, Rotten Tomato, Rotten Cherry |
| Spider, floors one and two | Spider's Eye, Spider's Legs |
| Spider, floors three and four | Spider's Eye, Spider's Silk, Spider's Legs |

The spiders up top give an eye or a leg, and only the deeper ones, from the third floor on, spin the valuable silk. Those deeper spiders also hunt you on sight now, where before they would only sit and wait for you to come to them.

## Where this stands

Most of the above is built and checked on our side. It builds clean, the data loads, and every dialog leads somewhere real. What it has not had yet is a player in the live game looking around, sipping a brandy to see it bite, reading the book at the inn, and walking the crypt floor by floor. That is the next job, and we will call out anything that misbehaves.

The deeper unfinished pieces are the same few we keep naming:

- Swimming can be learned but has no water to use it on yet, so it waits on a future event.
- Gear is still not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things far above them.
- The flat damage bonus printed on armor and trinkets is still not counted, pending a look at some wild imported numbers.
- The crypt below floor eight is still the rough old import, not yet brought up to the standard of the floors above it.
- Lock-picking, parcels through the post, the altar's offerings and weddings, and the rest of Mileth's quest chains are written down but not yet built.

Wander in and look around if you get the chance.
