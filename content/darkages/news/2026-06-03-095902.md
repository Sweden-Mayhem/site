# From the Path of Beginnings to the Mileth Inn

Most of this week went to the very start of the game, the tutorial, and to a sweep of fixes that touch every new character: skills and spells you can learn at the right time, shop shelves at sane prices, doorways you can actually walk through, and townsfolk wearing the right faces. Here is the run-down.

## The tutorial is a journey now

The opening used to be one crowded floor with every teacher lined up across it. It is a proper chain of rooms now. You wake on the Path of Beginnings, and the teachers live in their own houses off it: Myles the wizard, Gerard the gardener, and a brand new face, Tealor, an arms merchant who sells you your first weapon and shield and shows you how to equip them. You walk in through their doors and out again, the way you would expect.

A new character also starts the way they should. Instead of just the green starter clothes, you wake carrying an apple, two slices of bread, and a stick to equip, with a few lines of guidance along the top of the screen telling you what you are holding, how to move, and to follow the path East when you are ready.

Out on the Path itself you will meet Kardi, a harmless practice creature you can swing at while you find your feet. It will not fight back.

![Kardi, the tutorial practice creature](/darkages/images/kardi.png)

We also trimmed the opening down to the figures who belong there. A handful of stand-in helpers we had put on the map to fill it out are cleared off, and the two teachers who remain, Bartley and Raghnall, stand on their proper spots.

## And it ends the way it should

Finishing the tutorial is its own moment now. When you have learned what the teachers have to offer, you follow the path East to the edge of the map. A warning tells you this road runs one way, and then you awaken upstairs in a room of the Mileth Inn to a fading dream, a light swirling around you, before you head downstairs to meet Riona the innkeeper.

> That was some dream. It feels good to wake in the fresh straw bed of Mileth. Could it be? I am an Aisling!

Where an invented gatekeeper used to stand in the way, the real ending plays out instead.

## You can walk through doors

Here is a quiet one that had been wrong for a long time. Most building entrances in town were dead to anyone on foot. The doorway tiles read as solid walls to the server, so a warp that should have carried you inside never fired, and there was simply no walking into a shop. About eighteen of Mileth's entrances were like this, along with the tutorial houses above.

That is fixed in the engine. A player can now step onto a doorway and be carried inside, while monsters still treat the same tile as a wall so they do not go wandering through shop fronts. Walking into the weaponsmith, the bank, the tavern, or a tutorial house works now.

```
var openDoor = GetEntitiesAtPoints<Door>(point).FirstOrDefault(d => !d.Closed);
// a doorway is flagged as a wall in map data but is walkable to a player;
// let an aisling step onto a wall tile carrying a warp reactor so the warp fires
var isWarpDoorway = (collisionType == CreatureType.Aisling) && HasWarpReactorAtPoint(point);
if ((openDoor is null) && !isWarpDoorway)
    return false;
```

We have it covered with tests on our side. The last word is a player walking through each door in the live client, which is the next job.

## Learn your skills and spells at the right time

This is the big correction. Every learnable skill and spell was set to unlock far too late, by two to four times in a lot of cases, which left the early game feeling empty. They are pinned to their proper levels now.

A Monk throws their first Kick at level one instead of five. A Warrior learns Assault at two. And a Wizard's fire magic, Beag Srad, is a level one spell, with the ladder climbing from there: Srad at four, Mor Srad at twenty, Ard Srad at thirty-four.

![The fire spell Srad](/darkages/images/srad-fire.png)

The elements also ask for the right kind of strength now. Fire keys off your Strength, water off Wisdom, wind off Dexterity, and earth off Constitution, the genuine elemental leanings, instead of everything hanging on Intellect.

A couple of skills changed how they behave to match. Kick and High Kick are proper moves on a cooldown now, not free additions to your spacebar swing. And the close-in dagger strike we had called "Stab" carries its right name, Stab and Twist.

A few of the headline shifts, old level to new:

| Skill or spell | Was | Now |
|---|---|---|
| Kick (Monk) | 5 | 1 |
| Assault (Warrior) | 5 | 2 |
| Beag Srad (lesser fire) | 2 | 1 |
| Srad (fire) | 8 | 4 |
| Mor Srad (greater fire) | 41 | 20 |
| Ard Srad (astounding fire) | 75 | 34 |

The damage and mana figures are still our own balance and may shift once they are played in. It is the levels, costs, reagents, and stat requirements that are now set to their proper marks.

## Mileth's shops, at the right price

The town's shops were carrying bloated stock at nonsense prices, a wall of endgame blades on a starter weapon rack and rum that cost a small fortune. Six of them are trimmed to exactly what they should sell, at the right coin.

Walk into Callough's weaponsmith now and you see three plain choices:

![A Claidheamh](/darkages/images/claidheamh.png)

| Weapon | Price |
|---|---|
| Stick | 100 |
| Dirk | 500 |
| Claidheamh | 1,300 |

The tavern pours rum at 100 coins instead of twenty-five thousand, and the armorsmith, tailor, baker, and the dark wizard Dar all carry their right wares at their right prices too. A few items the shops should stock but we have not built yet, a plain shirt, some brandy, a parchment scroll, the gem family, are noted as gaps rather than faked in.

## The right faces

A smaller but satisfying fix: fifteen townsfolk and teachers were wearing the wrong body, a stray sprite carried in from the source data. Cian the alchemist, Riona the innkeeper, Aoife at the Hall of Advancement, the tutorial teachers, and others now all look the way they should. Every NPC in the game now wears its correct skin.

## Where this sits

Most of the above is built and checked on the server. It builds clean, the data loads, the maps connect, and every door and dialog leads somewhere real. What it has not had yet is a player walking the whole opening in the live client, door by door, and that is the next job.

The deeper unfinished pieces are the same as last time, and we would rather name them than tuck them away:

- A weapon's own damage and accuracy still do not feed into a fight, so your strength drives your hits for now. The last piece of that is a balance call sitting in front of the team.
- Attacks never miss yet. There is no dodge or saving throw in the math.
- Gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things they should not.
- The crypt below floor eight is still the raw old import, not yet brought up to the same standard as the floors above it.

All of it is on the list. The opening is the piece we are keenest to see played, so wander in and walk it if you get the chance.
