# Warriors swing, wizards burn, priests heal

The last update made dying no longer break your character. This one is about what you do while you are alive. Each of the five paths now has a real starter kit to learn at the Mileth trainer, the town around that trainer has been filled in, and the first floor of the crypt below it has been rebuilt from the ground up. Here is the run-down.

## Warriors get their swing

Before adding anything new, we went back and checked the four basic skills the game shipped with, because they were still carrying placeholder data. Assail, the universal basic attack every class starts with, checks out. Two-Handed Attack was leaking to everyone (Wizards included) and wore the wrong icon and sound; it is now correctly Warrior-only with its proper look. Ambush, the Monk strike, now flashes its real effect and plays its sound when it lands. Throw is still rough, and we have flagged it for a proper rebuild rather than pretend it is done.

On top of that cleanup, Warriors now have a real progression to learn from Kousai, the Mileth trainer: Assault, then Clobber, then Wallop, each a stronger extra attack, plus Wind Blade, a blade of force that cuts through a line of up to three foes in front of you. Each one is Warrior-only and costs the proper materials and coin:

| Skill | Level | Cost | Needs first |
|---|---|---|---|
| Assault | 5 | Centipede Gland + 500 coins | none |
| Clobber | 7 | Mantis Eye + 1,000 coins | Assail |
| Wind Blade | 11 | Bat Wing + 5,000 coins | Assail |
| Wallop | 20 | Stilla + Battle Sword + 10,000 coins | Assail, Clobber |
| Two-Handed Attack | 11 | Claidhmore + 5,000 coins | none |

## Wizards command the elements

A new Wizard reaching the trainer used to find almost nothing to learn but a deliberately feeble tutorial spell. Now the four classical elements are in: Srad (fire), Sal (water), Athar (air), and Creag (earth), each in two tiers. The lesser Beag form is learnable early, at level two for a small reagent, and the full form at level eight for a bit more, with its Beag form required first. Each one hurls an element-coloured bolt at a single target with the right cast motion and sound.

The reagents they ask for (centipede glands, spider eyes, bee wings) all drop in the crypt and the fields nearby, so the cost is something you can go out and gather rather than just buy.

## Priests can finally heal

Choosing Priest at level five used to leave you with nothing to learn but three leftover spells, one of which did nothing at all. That is fixed. Priests now have the start of their real kit:

- **Beag Ioc Fein**, **Beag Ioc**, **Ioc**, and **Beag Ioc Comlha**: the healing line, from mending yourself, to mending an ally, to a stronger heal, to a group heal that washes over a target and the companions around them.
- **Armachd**: the armour blessing that makes its target harder to hurt.
- **Beag Cradh**: the curse that weakens a foe's armour so they take more damage.
- **Ao Beag Cradh**: the cleansing prayer that lifts that curse.

The blessing and the curse are two sides of the same coin, just nudging a target's armour the right way for a while, and the cure simply ends the curse early:

```
Armachd        target armour improves by 10 while it lasts
Beag Cradh     target armour worsens by 20 while it lasts
Ao Beag Cradh  strips the Beag Cradh curse off a target
```

For now you learn all of this at Kousai alongside everyone else, the same stand-in arrangement we use for the Wizard line until the proper guild trainers are built.

### The bank

![Cassidy, the Mileth banker](/darkages/images/cassidy.png)

The Mileth banker is now **Cassidy**, standing just inside the door. You can deposit and withdraw both coins and items through her, into a vault that is yours alone and has no limit. And the bank wall now holds the **Mileth Merchant Board**, the public notice board where Aislings post what they want to buy and sell.

## Two quests to start you off

Mileth's people do not just sell things now. Two of the town's first quests are in:

- **A Little Bit of That**, from Cian. Bring him any spoiled food you turn up in the crypt (a rotten apple, some mold, spoiled grapes and the like) and he rewards 2,000 experience. Repeatable every twelve hours.
- **Spare a Stick**, from Callough the weaponsmith. Take his "Spare a stick?", then "I'll gather them now," and then go for a walk: branches start appearing in your pack as you wander the ground near the Mileth Inn and out along the east road. Bring six back (while carrying fewer than 1,500 coins) and he forges you a Stick and two Wooden Shields for 3,500 experience, with a chance at a bonus Oak Stick and the "Loved by Mileth Mundanes" mark on your legend. Repeatable every eight hours.

That second quest is also your first taste of a system we will lean on a lot: gathering. Walking the right ground to collect something is now a reusable piece of machinery, so the next time you are picking herbs, drawing raw honey, or mining ore, it will work the same way.

## Down into the crypt

![A centipede](/darkages/images/centipede.png)

The first dungeon a new Aisling hunts, Mileth Crypt floor one, is rebuilt. It is home to **Rats** and **Spiders**, both timid (they only fight back once you strike them), and **Centipedes**, which are not timid at all and will come for you on sight. The centipedes drop **Centipede Glands**, and that matters more than it sounds: the gland is both a reagent for those early fire spells above and something Dar pays good coin for. The rats and spiders drop the right rotting food and spider parts for the two quests above, so the loop closes: hunt the crypt, turn it in up top.

At the mouth of the crypt, the out-of-place sword merchant who used to stand there is gone, replaced by the proper vestibule keeper, **Felimid**, who warns you about what waits below and points you to who buys what you find.

## Where this sits

Most of the above was built and checked on the server: it builds clean, the data loads, and every dialog button leads somewhere real. The next job is to walk each of these through in the live game, double-clicking each NPC and casting each spell, and we will call out anything that misbehaves.

A few deeper things are still openly unfinished, and we would rather name them:

- A weapon's damage and accuracy numbers show on your stats, but they do not yet feed into combat, so for now it is your strength that drives your hits.
- Attacks never miss yet. There is no dodge or saving throw in the math.
- Gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can still wear things they have no business wearing.
- The crypt below floor one is still the raw import, not yet brought up to the same standard.

All of that is on the list.
