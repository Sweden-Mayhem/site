# Warriors swing, wizards burn, priests heal

Last time the big news was that dying no longer breaks your character. This window is about what you do while you are alive. Each of the five paths now has a real starter kit to learn at the Mileth trainer, the town around that trainer has been filled in to match the Mileth you remember, and the first floor of the crypt below it has been rebuilt from the ground up. Here is the run-down.

## Warriors get their swing

Before adding anything new, we went back and checked the four basic skills the game shipped with, because they were still carrying placeholder data from the engine's old demo. Assail, the universal basic attack every class starts with, checks out. Two-Handed Attack was leaking to everyone (Wizards included) and wore the wrong icon and sound; it is now correctly Warrior-only with its proper look. Ambush, the Monk strike, now flashes its real effect and plays its sound when it lands. Throw is still rough, and we have flagged it for a proper rebuild rather than pretend it is done.

On top of that cleanup, Warriors now have a real progression to learn from Kousai, the Mileth trainer: Assault, then Clobber, then Wallop, each a stronger extra attack, plus Wind Blade, a blade of force that cuts through a line of up to three foes standing in front of you. Each one is Warrior-only now and costs the proper materials and coin, taken from the old Warrior guides:

| Skill | Level | Cost | Needs first |
|---|---|---|---|
| Assault | 5 | Centipede Gland + 500 coins | none |
| Clobber | 7 | Mantis Eye + 1,000 coins | Assail |
| Wind Blade | 11 | Bat Wing + 5,000 coins | Assail |
| Wallop | 20 | Stilla + Battle Sword + 10,000 coins | Assail, Clobber |
| Two-Handed Attack | 11 | Claidhmore + 5,000 coins | none |

## Wizards command the elements

A new Wizard reaching the trainer used to find almost nothing to learn but a deliberately feeble tutorial spell. Now the four classical elements are in: Srad (fire), Sal (water), Athar (air), and Creag (earth), each in two tiers. The lesser Beag form is learnable early, at level two for a small reagent, and the full form at level eight for a bit more, with its Beag form required first. Each one hurls an element-colored bolt at a single target with the right cast motion and sound.

The reagents they ask for (centipede glands, spider eyes, bee wings) all drop in the crypt and the fields nearby, so the cost is something you can actually go out and gather rather than just buy.

## Priests can finally heal

Choosing Priest at level five used to leave you with nothing to learn but three leftover demo spells, one of which literally did nothing. That is fixed. Priests now have the start of their real kit:

- **Beag Ioc Fein**, **Beag Ioc**, **Ioc**, and **Beag Ioc Comlha**: the healing line, from mending yourself, to mending an ally, to a stronger heal, to a group heal that washes over a target and the companions around them.
- **Armachd**: the armour blessing that makes its target harder to hurt.
- **Beag Cradh**: the curse that weakens a foe's armour so they take more damage.
- **Ao Beag Cradh**: the cleansing prayer that lifts that curse.

The blessing and the curse are two sides of the same coin under the hood, just nudging a target's armour the right way for a while, and the cure simply ends the curse early:

```
Armachd        target armour improves by 10 while it lasts
Beag Cradh     target armour worsens by 20 while it lasts
Ao Beag Cradh  strips the Beag Cradh curse off a target
```

In retail, healing is Devlin's trade to teach; for now you learn all of this at Kousai alongside everyone else, the same stand-in arrangement we use for the Wizard line until the proper guild trainers are built.

Each shop now stocks goods that fit its trade (the bakery sells food, the tavern pours drinks, the smith sells blades), and every one of them finally has a polite way out: a "Farewell." that closes the conversation instead of leaving you stuck in the menu.

Two of them now buy what you carry up from the crypt. Cian the alchemist takes the reagents and rotting oddments you find below. And Dar, the dark wizard in the north corner of town, buys the grislier finds at the prices he always paid:

### The bank

The Mileth banker is now **Cassidy**, standing at her proper spot just inside the door. You can deposit and withdraw both coins and items through her, into a vault that is yours alone and has no limit. And the bank wall now holds the **Mileth Merchant Board**, the public notice board where Aislings post what they want to buy and sell, right where retail always told new players to leave their first posting.

## Two quests to start you off

Mileth's people do not just sell things now. Two of the town's first quests are in:

- **A Little Bit of That**, from Cian. Bring him any spoiled food you turn up in the crypt (a rotten apple, some mold, spoiled grapes and the like) and he rewards 2,000 experience. Repeatable every twelve hours.
- **Spare a Stick**, from Callough the weaponsmith. Take his "Spare a stick?", then "I'll gather them now," and then go for a walk: branches start appearing in your pack as you wander the ground near the Mileth Inn and out along the east road. Bring six back (while carrying fewer than 1,500 coins) and he forges you a Stick and two Wooden Shields for 3,500 experience, with a chance at a bonus Oak Stick and the cherished "Loved by Mileth Mundanes" mark on your legend. Repeatable every eight hours.

That second quest is also your first taste of a system we will lean on a lot: gathering. Walking the right ground to collect something is now a reusable piece of machinery, so the next time you are picking herbs, drawing raw honey, or mining ore, it will work the same familiar way.

## Down into the crypt

The first dungeon a new Aisling hunts, Mileth Crypt floor one, now matches the old beastiary. It is home to **Rats** and **Spiders**, both timid (they only fight back once you strike them), and **Centipedes**, which are not timid at all and will come for you on sight. The centipedes drop **Centipede Glands**, and that matters more than it sounds: the gland is both a reagent for those early fire spells above and something Dar pays good coin for, and until now it could not be found where retail puts it. The rats and spiders drop the right rotting food and spider parts for the two quests above, so the loop closes: hunt the crypt, turn it in up top.

At the mouth of the crypt, the out-of-place sword merchant who used to stand there is gone, replaced by the proper vestibule keeper, **Felimid**, who warns you about what waits below and points you to who buys what you find.

## Honest about where this sits

Most of the above was built and checked on the server: it builds clean, the data loads, and every dialog button leads somewhere real. But a lot of it still needs the one thing we cannot do from this side, a real player double-clicking each NPC and casting each spell in the live client. That click-test pass is the next job, and we will call out anything that misbehaves once it happens.

A few deeper things are still openly unfinished, and we would rather say so than hide them:

- A weapon's damage and accuracy numbers are shown on your stats, but they do not yet feed into combat, so for now it is your strength that drives your hits.
- Attacks never miss yet. There is no dodge or saving throw in the math.
- Gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can still wear things they have no business wearing.
- The crypt below floor one is still the raw old import, not yet brought up to the retail roster.

All of that is on the list. As always, if something here is marked working and it is not, tell us. That is exactly what we want to hear.
