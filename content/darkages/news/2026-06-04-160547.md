# The road reaches Abel

Last update opened the map of Temuair and the roads leading off it. This time we followed one of those roads all the way to its end: Abel, the second town, and made it a place you can actually walk through. Along with that, the deep crypt learned some nasty new tricks, the Mileth trainer has a few things to teach, and a lot of silly price tags came down.

## Abel, and a square you can walk through

Click "Abel" on the world map and you arrive on the Abel Port Way, the road into the second town. From there you can walk south into Abel Port itself, the busy square at the heart of the town.

![Abel Port, the town square](/darkages/images/abel-port.png)

We went over the gateway road and the square and set them right. The road no longer has a made-up guard standing on it pretending to belong, so it is the quiet stretch it should be, with the way south into the square, west to a shrine, and east back to the world map all working in both directions. The square needed more work. It used to be crowded with invented townsfolk, and every shop door was sealed. Now the strangers are gone and the doors open: walk into the bank, the tavern, the inn, the restaurant, the armorer, the goods store, the weaponsmith, the combat master's hall, the magic shop, the fish market, or out to the coast, and walk straight back out again. Before this, stepping into Abel Port left you on a square full of made-up guards with every door locked.

Where this stands, plainly: getting to Abel and moving around its square and through its doors works. The shops behind those doors are not dressed yet, so the keepers, their wares, and their talk will be rough or missing until we get to them. The sea route south out of Abel also waits on a stretch of world map we have not built, so for now you leave Abel the way you arrived, through the overworld.

## The crypt learns to bite

![A crypt Scorpion](/darkages/images/crypt-scorpion.png)

Until now the deeper crypt creatures could only bite you. This update gives three of them their signature tricks. A Scorpion's sting paralyzes you, locking your limbs. The Chest, the mimic that sits looking like treasure, lulls you to sleep with a drowsy "Zzz" floating over your head. And the Kardi's touch freezes you stiff. While you are held you cannot walk, turn, or call up a skill or spell for a few seconds, so the creature gets its free hits in. You can still talk and gulp a potion, and the hold cannot be chained on you forever, since there is always a guaranteed gap where you get to act again.

This needed a new piece of the engine, because nothing before could take you out of your own control like that. We built it as a reusable thing, so the groundwork is already laid for the players' own sleep and hold spells later on.

The Succubus's charm and the Wraith's bewitching are not in yet; those need a different sort of handle and we will come back for them. And while these new locks pass their tests on our side, we have not yet watched one land on a player in the live game, so if a freeze or a sleep does something odd, that is exactly the kind of thing to tell us.

## One way in, one way out

While we were down there, we walked every floor-to-floor doorway across all forty-six maps of the Mileth Crypt and found five that led somewhere they had no business leading. The worst of them: a tile in the Mileth Training Room, where you go to learn your skills, could dump you into the middle of crypt floor twenty-three, a floor meant for level twenty and up, and the trip ran both ways. Others jumped you from mid-crypt straight into the Mileth Inn.

All five are pulled out. The crypt is now a clean descent with a single mouth at the top connecting to the village, and nothing leaking between the dungeon and the safe corners of town. As a bonus, those broken doors had also been a route for a crypt monster to wander up into the inn, which can no longer happen.

## New things to learn

The Mileth trainer has a few new tricks on offer, and a stack of fakes came off the shelf.

![The Throw Surigum skill](/darkages/images/throw-surigum.png)

A Rogue can now learn **Throw Surigum** at level four, a thrown surigum that catches the nearest enemy a couple of tiles in front of you. It is the earliest scrap of damage a fresh Rogue can reach for, the one you lean on before the daggers come out. Throwing it drops you out of Hide.

![The Beag Puinsein spell](/darkages/images/beag-puinsein.png)

We also built the poison line. A Priest can learn **Beag Puinsein** at level eighteen and **Puinsein** at level thirty to lay a poison on a foe that eats away their health over time, the second lasting longer than the first. A Monk can learn **Ao Puinsein** at level five to purge poison from an ally or from themselves. The poison deals real damage now, credited to whoever cast it, so it can finish off a weakened enemy and the kill counts as yours.

And we cleared out the placeholders. The trainers used to list a few spells that were never real, a generic "poison," a "fire breath," and a couple of stand-in heals and curses left over from early scaffolding. They are gone from every teacher's list, so what you can learn is what belongs in the game.

The numbers on the new attacks, how hard the throw hits and how fast the poison bites, are our own first guess and still being felt out, so they may shift as we play with them.

## Fairer prices on the shelf

Prices got a real going-over. A lot of gear was wearing wildly inflated tags. The first place you will notice it is the tutorial arms merchant, Tealor: his Long Sword dropped from a frankly silly 45,000 gold to 12,200, and his Loures Saber from 12,000 to 1,500. The same correction flows through to every town weapon and armor seller that stocks those same items, forty-six pieces in all now set to their proper price.

| Item | Was | Now |
|---|---|---|
| Long Sword | 45,000 | 12,200 |
| Loures Saber | 12,000 | 1,500 |
| Black Death | 45,000 | 8,800 |
| Templar | 32,000 | 4,500 |

While we were in the gear, we went over the stats on the early Mileth shop equipment too, the shields, boots, gauntlets, and earrings you buy right after the tutorial, and fixed eight items whose numbers were off. A couple gated at the wrong level, several carried inflated durability, and the Leather Gauntlet was missing the mana penalty it is meant to come with. All squared up now.

## Smaller touches

The Hall of Advancement just outside Mileth, where you choose your path at level five, had the wrong attendant. A stranger with a vague "are you worthy" line is gone, and the proper greeter, Gregory, now stands there to welcome you and send you on to Aoife.

And the reagents and rotten food you loot in the crypt read correctly now, a Spider's Eye, a Centipede's Gland, a Bat's Wing, in place of the clipped spellings they used to carry.

## Where this stands

Most of the above is built and checked on our side. It builds clean, the data loads, the crypt seals up tight, the prices and stats line up, and the new holds pass their tests. What several of these pieces have not had yet is a player in the live game, watching a scorpion's sting land, walking Abel's shop doors, feeling out the poison. That is the next job.

The road ahead holds the familiar names. Abel's shops are reachable but not yet stocked or staffed. The crypt still wants the Succubus's charm, the Wraith's bewitching, and a closer look at its loot. And the towns past Mileth, lock-picking, crafting, and the rest of Mileth's quests are written down and waiting their turn.

Take the road to Abel if you get the chance, and mind the scorpions on the way down.
