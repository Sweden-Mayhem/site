# A sword that hits harder than a fist

This week's biggest change is one you will feel the moment you swing: the weapon in your hand finally drives your damage. Alongside it, food and drink actually do something when you use them, the opening got a guiding spirit and a real sense of place, and Mileth gained a row of new faces and a reason to haul junk up out of the crypt. Here is the run-down.

## A sword that hits harder than a fist

Until now your fists and a fine blade landed the same blow. A weapon showed its numbers in the stats panel, but the fight never read them, so your raw Strength did all the talking. That changes today. The weapon you hold adds its own damage range to every swing, and it climbs the further up the rack you go.

| Weapon | Level | Damage added |
|---|---|---|
| Stick | 1 | 4 to 14 |
| Dirk / Eppe | 2 | 4 to 14 |
| Loures Saber | 7 | 18 to 28 |
| Claidheamh | 14 | 18 to 28 |
| Long Sword | 56 | 52 to 72 |

![A Long Sword](/darkages/images/long-sword.png)

We also added the first miss to the maths. A well-armoured fighter can now slip a physical blow entirely. We tied it to your armour, and on purpose only a fighter geared deep into the protective end of the scale earns any dodge at all, so nothing in the early game suddenly starts whiffing. It is a reward for kitting out, not a tax on a beginner.

```csharp
// a physical skill can now be dodged outright by a well-armoured defender
if (script is ISkillScript && RollDodge(target))
    return 0;

// and the equipped weapon's own range is added to the swing, before armour
damage += Random.Shared.Next(weaponMin, weaponMax + 1);
```

The weapon ranges are deliberately modest, and the dodge scale is our own number that may move once it has been felt in a fight. Two things we will say plainly: the flat damage bonus printed on armour and trinkets still is not counted (some of the imported pieces carry wild figures we want to sanity-check first), and nothing yet stops a fresh character from strapping on gear far above their level.

## Food that feeds you, potions that heal

Every edible in the game used to be inert. You could double-click an apple all day and nothing happened. Now they do their job.

![An apple](/darkages/images/apple.png)

The orchard food restores a little health each:

| Food | Health |
|---|---|
| Cherry | 4 |
| Apple | 4 |
| Tomato | 6 |
| Baguette | 12 |
| Chicken | 16 |
| Beef | 20 |

The three healing draughts are the bigger gulp. The orange mends sixty health, the purple four hundred, and the red fills you to the brim.

![Orange healing potion](/darkages/images/potion-orange.png) ![Purple healing potion](/darkages/images/potion-purple.png) ![Red healing potion](/darkages/images/potion-red.png)

These amounts are the measured end of the scale on purpose. The fancier brews (cures, mana, the buff potions) and the spoiled things you should not eat are still inert and on the list.

## The opening, with a guide

The first steps got another good look. A new character now wakes carrying the whole starter outfit in their pack rather than already dressed: your attire, a fresh pair of boots, an apple, two slices of bread, and a stick. You dress yourself, which is the same lesson the stick was already teaching.

More than that, a guiding spirit now meets you in a proper dialog window when you wake, instead of a line of text along the top of the screen that is easy to miss. It tells you what you are carrying and where to head. When you finish and walk East to the edge of the map, the spirit asks whether you are sure, because that road runs one way. Say you are ready and you wake upstairs in the Mileth Inn, the dream fading and a light swirling, before you step down to meet Riona. The dream-only fire spell you practised with does not survive the waking.

> That was some dream. Could it be? I am an Aisling!

A couple of small fixes rode along. The two teachers who point you onward were still sending you to a gate that no longer stands, and they now tell you to follow the path East as they should. And the inn stairs drop you right beside Riona instead of across the room.

One more touch for everyone, not just new characters: levelling up now plays a short chime over the burst of light, so the moment actually lands.

## Mileth keeps filling in

The starter town gained a clutch of the people who belong in it.

**Daithi**, the special-skill master, stands in his hall now and sells the one tool a budding rogue needs. We built the proper Lockpicks item from scratch.

![Lockpicks](/darkages/images/lockpicks.png)

Fair warning: lock-picking itself, the locked chests and the skills that use a pick, is not built yet, so for now a set of picks is a tool waiting for its job.

**Colm the courier** keeps the Mileth post office and will explain how the mail works. The restaurant and tavern are full of the right characters too: Oona behind the food counter, the barmaid Aingeal, Duana who dances, Teague the beggar with his rumours, and Theradus, who speaks of a sword made from dragon scale. The weaponsmith and the armorer also shifted onto the exact spots they should hold. In every one of these rooms a few strangers carried in by the old import were sent off to where they actually live.

The most useful change for a thin purse: **Cian the alchemist** is a buyer now, not a seller. He pays real coin for the spoiled food and oddments the crypt vermin drop, where before that junk sold for nothing at all. Clear rats and spiders down in the crypt, carry the spoils up to Cian, and you have your earliest steady income.

| You bring Cian | He pays |
|---|---|
| Rotten Apple | 25 |
| Rotten Grapes | 50 |
| Rotten Vegetable | 250 |
| Mantis's Eye | 1,500 |

And three of Mileth's masters now reward you simply for listening. Torrance the armorer, Devlin the White Magic Master, and Keallach the Combat Skill Master each give a lecture in their own hall off the square, seven thousand experience in all for hearing them out. Two of those masters had been stranded on the wrong maps and could not be reached at all before.

## Where this stands

Most of the above is built and checked on our side. It builds clean, the data loads, every dialog leads somewhere real, and the new combat maths is covered by tests. What it has not had yet is a player swinging a sword in the live game, eating an apple, and walking the whole opening door by door. That is the next job, and we will flag anything that misbehaves.

The deeper unfinished pieces are the same few we keep naming:

- Gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things far above them.
- The flat damage bonus on armour and trinkets is still not counted, pending a look at some wild imported numbers.
- The crypt below floor eight is still the rough old import, not yet brought up to the standard of the floors above it.
- Lock-picking, parcels through the post, and the rest of Mileth's quest chains are written down but not yet built.

Wander in and swing something if you get the chance.
