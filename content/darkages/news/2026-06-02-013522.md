# You awaken in the Mileth Inn

There is a small moment every old Dark Ages player remembers without quite meaning to. You stumble out of the tutorial, the screen settles, and there is the Mileth Inn, warm and a little crowded, with the innkeeper looking up to greet you. That moment is back.

When you finish the tutorial now, you wake up inside the Mileth Inn proper, not on the street outside it, and Riona the innkeeper is right there to welcome you. Speak with her and she does what she always did. She tells you that you have left your mundane life behind and become an Aisling, free to make your own way, and she nudges you to find a guide and go see the world. For the trouble of saying hello she hands you five hundred experience, the old first-steps reward.

She also keeps her other little ritual. Ask her about your mundane birth and she will walk you through it: which town you spent your mundane years in, and the sun and moon you were born under. Tell her true and she writes it into your legend forever, a small line of who you were before the spark took you. Be warned, as she warns you, that once it is set it is not meant to be unmade.

A note on names, since a few of you have sharp memories: Riona is the innkeeper of Mileth, full stop. We had briefly hung her name on the wrong shop, and that is fixed now, so there is only one Riona in town and she is exactly where she should be.

This is a quiet feature, but it is the kind of quiet that matters. It is the first face of the real game after the training wheels come off, and getting that handshake right sets the tone for everything after it. Next we keep working down the Mileth list: the townsfolk who should have their own things to say, the first real quests, and the path that leads down into the crypt. The inn keeper is home. The town is starting to wake up around her.
