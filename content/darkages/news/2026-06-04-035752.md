# The road out of Mileth

Mileth has been the whole world for a while now. This update opens the gate: walk east past the village and the map of Temuair unfolds, with roads to a dozen places beyond the starter town. Alongside that, your gear finally has to fit you, the crypt runs far deeper than it did, and a new master sets up to teach the elements.

## Walk east, and the world opens

Until now, Mileth was an island. You could walk its square, its shops, and its crypt, and that was the edge of everything. No longer.

Head east out of town onto Mileth Village Way, follow the road to its far edge, and the map of Temuair opens in front of you. Click a place and you travel there:

- Abel
- the Western and Eastern Woodlands
- Pravat Cave
- Piet
- the Kasmanium Mines
- Loures and the Loures Harbor
- Rionnag
- and Mileth itself, whenever you want to come back

Every destination has its own portal on its edge to bring you home, so nowhere is a one-way trap.

One word of caution, since we would rather say it than let you find out the hard way. Travel works, but most of these places are still rough. The roads reach them; the towns themselves are next on the list, so expect plenty of empty rooms and unfinished corners when you step off the map. Getting there is the part that is new.

## Gear that has to fit you

This is one we have named as missing in nearly every one of these updates, and it is finally done. Up to now a brand new character could strap on anything in the game, endgame armor and weapons included, whether dug out of a chest or handed over by a friend. That is over.

Every piece of equipment now carries a level and, past the basic peasant set, a class. Try to wear something you have not grown into and the game tells you so: it "does not seem to fit you, but you could grow into it." A Wizard's robe will not go on a Warrior, and a sword meant for level fifty stays in your pack until you are level fifty. The starter gear every Aisling needs, the Stick, the Dirk, your green attire, stays usable from level one, exactly as it should.

| Item | You need |
|---|---|
| Stick | level 1 |
| Dirk | level 2 |
| Claidheamh | level 14 |
| Oak Staff | level 19, Wizard only |
| Long Sword | level 56 |

The engine already knew how to enforce this; the gear simply had no requirements attached to it. We pulled the real numbers in for every piece and covered the gate with tests, so it holds.

## Deeper into the crypt

Last time we rebuilt the top of the Mileth Crypt, floors one through eight. This time we carried the work all the way down to floor thirty.

The deep crypt now has a proper cast, each creature turning up on the floors it belongs to, at a sensible early-to-mid power, hunting and fighting back, with the magical ones throwing a bolt of darkness.

We also went back over the entry floors. The Centipede on the first two floors no longer hunts a fresh arrival on sight; like the mice and spiders beside it, it only fights back once you strike it. And the treasure Chest now does what a chest should, spilling a fat pile of gold when you crack it open, in place of the oddments it was wrongly dropping before.

## A master of the dark arts

Dar, the wizard in his hut just off the Mileth square, used to only hand out a single errand. Now he does what his sign says. His hut reads "Mileth Black Magic Master," and he teaches.

![Dar, the Black Magic Master](/darkages/images/dar.png)

A Wizard can walk in and learn his elemental spells, fire, water, wind, and earth, in their lesser and base forms. A Priest can learn a first curse, Beag Cradh. Dar still sells a blank Parchment Scroll, still buys the reagents you carry up from the crypt, and still runs his errand for anyone who wants it.

## Smaller touches

A handful of quieter fixes rode along this week.

Every item in the game now shows a plain, factual line when you examine it: what it is, its damage or stats, and the level or class it asks for. Before, all but a couple dozen items showed a blank panel.

The sixteen Wizard elemental spells each play their own cast sound now. A Mor Srad no longer sounds exactly like a Beag Srad, and fire no longer sounds like earth, where before all sixteen shared one placeholder whoosh.

We went over every skill and spell against our records and set a few right: the Priest's Cradh and Mor Cradh curses now bite at their proper strength, the Rogue's Hide and Needle Trap cost what they should to learn, and a couple of wrong icons and a stray cooldown were corrected.

Finishing the tutorial now hands out the right reward: a Dirk, a Small Emerald Ring, a Small Spinel Ring, a thousand coins, and five thousand experience. The Dirk also stopped quietly draining fifty of your maximum mana, a leftover penalty that was crippling a new character's tiny pool the moment they picked up their first real weapon.

And the road out of town lost a guard who had no business standing on it, so Mileth Village Way is the quiet stretch of road it ought to be.

## Where this stands

Most of the above is built and checked on our side. It builds clean, the data loads, the gear gate is covered by tests, and every dialog leads somewhere real. The one piece we have not yet watched with our own eyes in the live game is the world map itself drawing on screen with its destinations in the right spots. The travel underneath it works either way; it is the picture we still want to confirm.

The pieces ahead are the ones we keep naming. The towns the new roads reach are mostly empty for now. The deep crypt still needs its special attacks, the sleeps and freezes and charms, and a closer look at its lower stairwells. And lock-picking, crafting, the altar's offerings, and the rest of Mileth's quest chains are written down but not yet built.

Wander east if you get the chance, and tell us if the road leads somewhere strange.
