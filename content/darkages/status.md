# What works, what doesn't

We are rebuilding a big old game one piece at a time, and we would rather be honest about it than pretend everything is finished. This page is the running list of where things stand. It changes as we go, so check back.

Our yardstick is simple. A thing is "working" only when it behaves the way you remember from the real Dark Ages. If it is half built, or close but not right, it lives under "being worked on" or "not yet" instead of getting quietly faked.

---

## Working now

- Logging in, making a character, and walking the world
- Fighting monsters, taking loot, and gaining levels
- Mileth's shops, which buy and sell, with keepers and wares that match the town you remember, and every one of them now has a polite way out instead of trapping you in the menu
- Selling what you find: the alchemist Cian and the dark wizard Dar both buy the reagents and oddments you carry up from the crypt, at the prices they always paid
- The Mileth bank, where Cassidy keeps both your gold and your items safe in a vault of your own with no limit, with the Merchant Board back up on the wall
- Choosing your path (Warrior, Rogue, Wizard, Priest, Monk) once you reach level five
- Waking in the Mileth Inn when you leave the tutorial, where Riona the innkeeper greets you and records your mundane birth onto your legend
- Dying, and waking back up. Fall in battle and you take the chill of Sgrios, lose a little experience, and return to the Mileth temple at full health, the way you should, instead of being stuck as a ghost forever
- Your first quests: Gerard's floppy hunt in the tutorial garden, plus Cian's "A Little Bit of That" and Callough's "Spare a Stick" in Mileth, the last of which has you walk the ground to gather branches
- The first floor of the Mileth Crypt, rebuilt to its old self: timid rats and spiders, centipedes that hunt you on sight, and the loot they should drop (including the centipede gland the early spells and quests need)
- Reading and posting on the town message boards that are up so far (the Mileth Village board, the new Mileth Merchant Board inside the bank, and the tutorial grey slate)

---

## Being worked on

- **The tutorial.** It is now the real opening, not a stand-in. All five teachers are in place: Raghnall (combat), Bartley (knowledge), Myles (who grants the practice spell Srad Tut), Eadaoin (groups and the apple shop), and Keelin (signs and the grey slate). Gerard the gardener gives the first quest, the practice field is safe and tidy, and the gate out to Mileth works. Still to come: Flynn the basement trainer, the proper multi-room building, and the Tutorial Book reader.
- **Mileth.** The shops, bank, trainer, inn, and class temple all work, the shopkeepers carry their real names and trades, and the first town quests are in. Every door, shop entrance and the stairs down into the crypt have been checked against the old map and land exactly where they should. Still to come: townsfolk with their own lines, the rest of the town's quest chains, and the Temple of Choosing in the corner of town (for now you dedicate your path at the church).
- **The Mileth Crypt.** Floor one and its vestibule are rebuilt to match the old beastiary, down to which creatures are timid and which hunt you. The floors below it are still the raw import, with placeholder names and creatures not yet checked against where they belong. Bringing the rest of the crypt up to the same standard is next.
- **Skills and spells.** This grew a lot this stretch. Warriors now have their early melee line (Assault, Clobber, Wallop, Wind Blade, and a corrected Two-Handed Attack), Wizards their four elements in the lesser and base forms (Srad, Sal, Athar, Creag), and Priests their first heals, an armour blessing, and a curse with its cure, all learnable at the Mileth trainer. That is still a small slice of the hundreds retail has, and most paths, tiers, and proper guild trainers are still to come. The newest of these have been built and checked on the server but not yet tested by a real player in the client.
- **The fuller death system.** The basics work, but a friend reviving you on the spot with a Red Potion, the visible ghost and skull, and a death pile of dropped items are still to come.
- **Monster stats and behavior.** Tuned toward a retail feel rather than the inflated source numbers, but not yet matched monster by monster, and attacks never miss yet (no dodging or saving throws).
- **Groups, mail, and the interface.** All handled and working on the server. What remains is the live two-player, in-client test that only a pair of real clients can do.
- **Equipment.** It equips and the stats matter, but a weapon's damage and accuracy do not yet feed into combat (your strength drives your hits for now), and gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things they should not.

## Not yet

- Most quests, from the rest of the Mileth chains to the long roads later on
- The towns beyond Mileth, and the deeper crypt floors and dungeons past it
- Crafting, mining, guilds, reputation, and nation citizenship
- The overworld field maps that link the towns together
- The rest of the world's message boards
- Endgame zones and seasonal events
- Daily draws, chests, and the other old extras (which we intend to give to everyone, with no paywall)

---

## The client

You play through our own client, not the official one, and we are actively rebuilding it to be more modern. It already runs at any window size with a cleaner interface, and it runs happily under Wine on Linux and Mac. This is its own ongoing project alongside the world rebuild, so expect it to keep improving from one release to the next.

---

## How to read this

Think of the whole game as a checklist that runs from your first step in the tutorial to the deepest endgame. We are walking it in order, turning "not yet" into "working" as we go. When something is rough, it is on the list, and we will get to it.

Found something broken that is marked working here? Tell us. That is exactly the kind of thing we want to know.
