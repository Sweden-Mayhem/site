# What works, what doesn't

We are rebuilding a big old game one piece at a time, and we would rather tell you plainly where it stands than pretend it is finished. This page is the running list. It moves as we do, so look in again now and then.

The rule we hold ourselves to is simple. A thing counts as working only when it behaves the way it should in a real, finished game. If it is half built, or close but not quite right, it sits under being worked on or not yet instead of getting quietly faked.

---

## Working now

- Logging in, making a character, and walking the world
- Fighting monsters, taking their loot, and climbing levels
- Walking into the buildings. Step into a shop or house doorway and you go inside, where before most town entrances were shut to anyone on foot
- Mileth's shops, which buy and sell with the right keepers behind the counters, each now stocking exactly the goods it should at sensible prices instead of a wall of overpriced endgame gear, and every one with a polite way out of the conversation
- Selling your finds: the alchemist Cian and the dark wizard Dar both buy the reagents and oddments you carry up from the crypt, at the prices they are meant to pay
- The Mileth bank, where Cassidy keeps both your gold and your items in a vault of your own with no limit, the Merchant Board up on the wall beside her
- Choosing your path (Warrior, Rogue, Wizard, Priest, Monk) once you reach level five, with Aoife at the Hall of Advancement just outside Mileth
- Waking in the Mileth Inn when you finish the tutorial, where Riona the innkeeper greets you and records your mundane birth onto your legend
- Dying. Fall in battle and you take the chill of Sgrios, lose a little experience, and revive at the Mileth temple at full health, rather than being stuck as a ghost with no way out
- Your first quests: Gerard's floppy hunt in the tutorial garden, plus Cian's "A Little Bit of That" and Callough's "Spare a Stick" in Mileth, the last of which has you walk the ground to gather branches
- Reading and posting on the message boards that are up so far: the Mileth Village board, the Mileth Merchant Board inside the bank, and the tutorial grey slate

## Being worked on

- **The tutorial.** It is a real walkable opening now, not one crowded floor. You wake on the Path of Beginnings carrying the right starter kit (an apple, two slices of bread, a stick to equip, and your green attire), with a few lines of guidance along the top of the screen. The teachers live in their own houses off the Path, each reached through its door: Myles the wizard, Gerard the gardener, and a new arms merchant, Tealor, who sells your first weapon and shield. A harmless creature called Kardi wanders the Path for you to practice on. When you are ready you follow the path East off the edge of the map and awaken upstairs in the Mileth Inn, then head down to meet Riona. Still to come: moving the teachers into the proper interior rooms and basement, the Tutorial Book reader, and a full walk-through of the whole opening in the live client.
- **Mileth.** The shops, bank, trainer, inn, and class choice all work, and this week the shops were trimmed to exactly the stock they should carry at the right prices. Every keeper now wears the right name and the right face, and the square holds the people who actually live there, with the made-up townsfolk and stray guards cleared out. The town's White Magic Master, Devlin, stands in the square and offers her "On Honey" quest, though the woodland bees it sends you to cannot yet be reached by road. Still to come: the rest of the town's quest chains, a handful of civic faces (a courier, temple and hall attendants, the rogue trainer), and a few shop items we have not built yet.
- **Skills and spells.** Every one of the five paths has an opening kit to train at the Mileth trainer: the Warrior's melee line, the Rogue's dagger work plus Hide and Needle Trap, the Monk's Kick line, the Wizard's four elements across the lesser-to-astounding ladder, and the Priest's prayers of healing and curse-breaking. This week we set them all to unlock at their proper levels, which had been far too high, and gave each element the right stat behind it (fire on Strength, water on Wisdom, wind on Dexterity, earth on Constitution). That is still a small slice of the hundreds the game holds, and most trees, tiers, and the proper class guild trainers are still ahead. The newest of these are built and checked on the server but not yet tried by a player in the client.
- **The Mileth Crypt.** Floors one through eight are rebuilt to the old beastiary: timid rats and spiders up top, centipedes that hunt on sight, then bats, scorpions, and great bats as you descend, each with the loot it should drop. The bats are the first creatures in our world to cast a spell at you, a bolt of darkness. The floors below eight are still the raw import, with placeholder names and creatures not yet checked against where they belong. Bringing them up to the same standard is next.
- **The fuller death system.** The basics work. A friend reviving you on the spot with a Red Potion, the visible ghost and skull, and a death pile of dropped items are still to come.
- **Monster stats and behavior.** Tuned toward a measured feel rather than the inflated source numbers, but not yet matched creature by creature. Some monsters now cast (the crypt bats), but fear and flight and the special debuffs are still missing, and attacks never miss yet.
- **Groups, mail, and the interface.** Handled and working on the server. What is left is the live two-player, in-client test that only a pair of clients can give us.
- **Equipment.** It equips and the stats count, but a weapon's own damage and accuracy do not yet feed into a fight (your strength drives your hits for now), and gear is not gated by level or class, so a fresh character can wear things they should not.

## Not yet

- Most quests, from the rest of the Mileth chains to the long roads later on
- The towns past Mileth, and the deeper crypt floors and dungeons beyond them
- Crafting, the wider mining and gathering, guilds, reputation, and nation citizenship
- The overworld field maps that link the towns together, which is what keeps a few built quests, like On Honey, from being walkable yet
- The rest of the world's message boards
- Endgame zones and seasonal events
- Daily draws, chests, and the other old extras, which we mean to give to everyone with no paywall

---

## How to read this

Think of the whole game as a checklist that runs from your first step in the tutorial to the deepest endgame. We are walking it in order, turning "not yet" into "working" as we go. When something is rough, it is on the list, and we will get to it.

Found something broken that is marked working here? Tell us. That is exactly the kind of thing we want to know.
